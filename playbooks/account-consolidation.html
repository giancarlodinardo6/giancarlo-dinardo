Playbook

Your account structure is part of your creative strategy.

At some point, most accounts stop making sense. Performance moves, but it's hard to explain why. It's not always the creative. Sometimes it's the structure changing how that creative is read.

This is where things usually break

Accounts don’t get messy overnight. They accumulate decisions.

Old campaigns stay live. New ones get layered on top. Tests never fully close. Over time, different parts of the account start competing with each other.

That’s when good creatives start looking worse than they actually are.

The issue isn’t volume. It’s fragmentation.

More campaigns can feel like more control. In practice, it often spreads signal too thin.

Instead of learning faster, the account hesitates. It struggles to decide what deserves spend.

What consolidation actually does

This isn’t about cleaning things up for the sake of it.

It’s about making sure the account can clearly see what’s working.

  • Reduce overlap between similar audiences
  • Group spend around real angles, not campaign logic
  • Let strong creatives get enough data to prove themselves

Where people get it wrong

A cleaner account isn’t automatically a better one.

If you merge things that are actually different, you lose signal.

The goal is not fewer campaigns. The goal is clearer learning.

Key takeaway

Structure doesn’t just organize the account.

It changes what the account is able to learn.
