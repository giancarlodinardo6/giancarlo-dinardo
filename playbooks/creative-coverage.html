Playbook · Coverage

Creative coverage is the only growth lever that compounds.

Bid strategy, audiences and budgets all have ceilings. Coverage is the one input that keeps paying off the more you invest in it, because every new asset gives the algorithm another signal to scale into.

By Giancarlo Di Nardo · 6 min read

One winner is a single point of failure

A single proven creative feels like safety. It isn't. The moment it fatigues, the whole account drops with it because there's nothing behind it to take the load. You can squeeze more out of bids and budgets for a while, but those levers tap out. A creative pipeline doesn't. The more good assets you put into rotation, the more room the algorithm has to keep spend efficient as it grows.

Coverage is what lets you scale spend without frequency quietly turning into a ceiling.

Separate testing from scaling

The most common mistake is running tests and scale inside the same campaigns, where a new idea either gets starved of budget or pollutes a structure that was working. Keep them apart. One side of the account exists to answer questions: isolate a variable, read it cleanly, retire it. The other side exists to put money behind what already converts. Coverage is the bridge between them, the steady supply of validated assets moving from the test side to the scale side.

Coverage is structural, not creative volume

More ads is not coverage. Coverage means every meaningful audience and every stage of intent has creative built for it, so no part of the account is running on a borrowed message. That's why it starts with a blueprint: map the layers and personas first, then build the few directions each one actually needs. Done that way, a dozen assets can cover an account that a hundred random variations would leave full of gaps.
